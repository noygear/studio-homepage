1Introduction and Scope

This Privacy Policy explains how Noygear AI Inc. and its affiliates, together Noygear, we, or us, handle information when you use the Noygear operations-management platform, our websites at noygear.ai and related domains, our applications, and our AI features, together the Platform. It uses the same defined terms as our Terms and Conditions.

This policy covers United States users. Where we handle records about the people you serve on your behalf, we do so under your instructions and under the agreement between us, and this policy explains that role in Section 12.

3How We Use Information

We use information to do the following.

  • Provide, operate, secure, and support the Platform, and do what you ask us to do.
  • Power AI Features, such as drafting, summarization, preparation, and triage.
  • Maintain safety and security, prevent fraud and abuse, and meet legal duties.
  • Improve the Platform, including by creating De-identified Data and Aggregated Data as described in Section 4.
  • Communicate with you about the Platform.

We use your Personal Information only for these operating purposes and for creating De-identified and Aggregated Data where the law permits. We do not use your Personal Information to train our own AI models.

4De-identification and Aggregation

Much of the Platform's value comes from data that does not identify anyone. We create two kinds.

Aggregated Data is statistics computed across many Users or subjects, such as benchmarks, indices, and counts. No individual record leaves. This is our primary method, because it carries the lowest re-identification risk and it works even for small operators.

De-identified Data is record-level data with identifiers removed under a documented method, which we use only where the pool is large and varied enough that a person cannot reasonably be singled out. Where a dataset is small or distinctive, we release only Aggregated Data, or we wait until the pool is large enough. For regulated data, we apply the de-identification standard that the regime requires.

We commit not to attempt to re-identify De-identified Data, except to test that our de-identification works, and we require the same of anyone we share it with. Once data is genuinely de-identified or aggregated, it is no longer Personal Information under applicable law, and we use and license it as described in Sections 5 and 6.

5Data Commercialization and the No-Sale Promise

We do not sell your Personal Information. We say that plainly and we mean it. De-identified Data and Aggregated Data are not Personal Information, and we use and license them to build and improve products, as described in Section 6.

We do not share your Personal Information for cross-context behavioral advertising. If that ever changes, we will update this policy and offer you the opt-out described in Section 10, as the California Consumer Privacy Act and similar laws require.

Our intent. Our intent is to protect what identifies you and the people you serve, and to use the rest to improve the Platform and create value for you and for everyone who uses it. Every use of the derivative layer is tied to a benefit you can see, such as better benchmarks, better AI features, and shared operational insight.

6Benchmarks and Data Products

From De-identified Data and Aggregated Data, we build and may license products, including the following.

  • Cross-operator benchmarks and operational indices, such as time-to-resolution, throughput, cost, and outcome baselines that no single operator can compute.
  • Counterparty and vendor performance signal, aggregated across operators.
  • Operational templates and workflows derived from patterns across the Platform.
  • Aggregated reliability signal, where a deployment has connected devices, which we may share with makers and suppliers.

None of these products include your Personal Information or any individual record.

7AI and Automated Decision-Making

7.1In-product AI features

Our AI Features may use third-party AI providers to generate a response. When they do, your inputs are sent to that provider only to produce the output for your session. Under those providers' terms, they do not use your inputs to train their own models. AI Feature outputs can be wrong, and you are responsible for reviewing them before you act.

7.2Training our own models

Separately from the in-product features, we train, test, validate, and improve our own artificial-intelligence and machine-learning models. This training uses only De-identified Data and Aggregated Data. It never uses your Personal Information. This use survives the end of your account, as explained in Section 9, because the data it draws on is no longer Personal Information. This is a separate activity from the in-product feature processing in Section 7.1, and we keep the two distinct.

7.3Automated decisions

The Platform supports your decisions. It is not designed to make legally significant decisions about a person on its own, and you remain responsible for the decisions you make using it.

8How We Share Information

We share information in these ways only.

  • Service providers. Vendors who host, process, or support the Platform under contract, and only to provide their service to us.
  • At your direction. Recipients you choose, such as when you send a document or share a record.
  • De-identified and Aggregated Data. As described in Sections 5 and 6.
  • Legal and safety. Where the law requires it, or to protect rights, safety, and the integrity of the Platform.
  • Business transfers. In a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, subject to this policy.

We do not share your Personal Information with third parties for their own marketing.

9Data Retention

We keep Personal Information for as long as your account is active and as long as we need it for the purposes in this policy, and then we return or delete it, subject to legal retention and to standard backup cycles. Where we act as a service provider or business associate, the agreement between us governs return and deletion.

De-identified Data and Aggregated Data, and the benchmarks, models, and products built from them, are not Personal Information and are not deleted when your account ends. Data already used to train or improve a model remains part of that model.

10Your Rights and Choices

Subject to applicable United States law, you have the right to know what Personal Information we hold about you, to access and correct it, to delete it, to opt out of any sale or share and of cross-context behavioral advertising, and to limit the use of Sensitive Personal Information. We do not discriminate against you for using these rights.

To make a request, contact us at privacy@noygear.ai. We will verify your request as the law requires. These rights apply to Personal Information. They do not extend to De-identified Data or Aggregated Data, which cannot be linked back to you, and they are subject to our legal retention duties and to the limits of what we can locate.

Where you are an operator and the request concerns Subject Data we handle on your behalf, we will refer the request to you or support you in answering it, as the agreement between us provides.

11Security

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect information, including encryption in transit, access controls, and logging. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. Tell us at security@noygear.ai if you believe your account or data has been compromised.

12Regulated Data and Our Role

Some deployments involve regulated data, such as health or financial records. Where that is the case, the applicable regime governs, and additional terms may apply, including a data processing agreement or a business associate agreement.

In those deployments we may act as your service provider or processor, or as a business associate. That means we handle the regulated Personal Information only to provide the Platform to you and for the other purposes the agreement and the law allow. It also means our own use of that data is limited to creating De-identified Data and Aggregated Data where the regime permits, for example de-identification of health data under 45 CFR 164.514. Regimes that can apply include HIPAA, the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, FERPA, 42 CFR Part 2, and their state equivalents. Where the agreement conflicts with this policy for that regulated data, the agreement controls.

13Jurisdiction

This policy and the Platform are intended for United States users. We do not offer the Platform to data subjects in the European Union or the United Kingdom under this policy. If Noygear later serves EU or UK users, a separate, GDPR-compliant policy will govern them, and the data model in Sections 4 through 9 does not apply to them.

14Children's Privacy

The Platform is for business and professional operators and is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect their Personal Information from them directly. Where you upload Subject Data that concerns minors in the course of your work, you are responsible for having the authority and consents the law requires, as set out in the Terms.

15Changes to this Policy

We may update this policy. When we make a material change, we will update the Last Updated date, raise the version, and give reasonable notice. Your continued use after a change takes effect means you accept the updated policy.

For privacy questions or to make a request, contact us at privacy@noygear.ai.
