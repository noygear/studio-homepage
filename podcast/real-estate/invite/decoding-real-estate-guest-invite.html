About the Podcast

Decoding Real Estate is a podcast about how the people who actually run buildings put technology to work — owners, operators, and property managers surfacing the decisions and tradeoffs behind the tools they adopt, giving working teams concrete playbooks, encouraging leaders still on the fence about new technology, and letting listeners hear how a sharp operator thinks before they ever sit across the table from one.

The show also serves the guest: it introduces you more deeply to peers, owners, boards, vendors, and future hires, and invites those partners on as co-hosts to deepen those relationships.

Our Guests Include

The leaders shaping how real estate runs, and the partners and voices around them:

  • Owners and operators — principals, asset managers, and portfolio leaders at management companies, ownership groups, and REITs who set the technology agenda across their buildings.
  • Property and building managers — the people on the phone all day with leaks, staff, boards, vendors, and residents, who decide which tools actually earn their place in the workflow.
  • Partners — brokers, insurance and risk advisors, construction and capital-projects leaders, attorneys, and the consultants who sit alongside every owner.
  • Proptech builders and thought leaders — founders and product leaders in building operations, resident experience, and compliance, plus analysts and authors covering the industry.

Why You Would Make a Great Guest

As an owner, operator, or property manager, you’ve seen the realities of putting technology to work from the inside — what works, what quietly costs more than it saves, and how it changes the experience for your team, your boards, and your residents. That perspective is exactly what our audience needs.

Our audience is made up of operators and managers navigating new technology for the first or hundredth time, owners weighing how their portfolio is run, and the partners and advisors who quietly shape every building’s path. They come to this show for the candor and depth that don’t usually make it into a conference panel or a vendor demo.

By sharing your story — your path, the bets that shaped how you operate, and the calls you’d make differently now — you’ll help fellow managers feel less alone and help peers feel less crazy for noticing the same broken pieces. This isn’t a hype reel and it isn’t a vendor webinar. It’s a forum for the people who actually run buildings to talk candidly about what technology is doing to the work.

Potential Topics

We’ll personalize these to you and pick three or four together on the prep call.

The operator’s view

  • What managers get wrong when adopting a new tool — and what the first 90 days should look like

The human behind the work
